Does altitude affect flavor?

Use the visualization below to explore how altitude affects the different tasting attributes of coffee.

  • Use the Coffee Grade filter to choose different grades of specialty coffee
  • Use the map to choose specific countries to filter the data.
    Note: that you can select multiple countries by holding CTRL. To select all, click on any whitespace.

    • Insight: Altitude affects one dimension, but coffee quality is much more complex

    The Altitude by Total Cup Points view shows all the coffee that had been professionally graded by certified Q-graders in the years between 2008 and 2018. This view is helpful in gaining an overview of a general correlation between the total Q score of a coffee in relation to its altitude.

    Overall, it shows that there is a slightly positive correlation of points vs altitude. This is supported by the following information

    • Overall, for acidity, aftertaste, and balance, the scatterplots show that there is a positive correlation between each of these attributes in relation to altitude
    • For sweetness, the data shows a slightly negative correlation, but with low confidence.

    The number of coffee farms and plantations vary per country and thus the average of the altitude may not be a good measure. However, the Average Altitude by Country view helps to narrow down which countries produce a coffee that's most appealing to a type of consumer.
