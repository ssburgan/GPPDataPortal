Will climate change affect coffee production?

A warming planet

Arabica represents 70 percent of the world’s coffee production and grows in a cool subtropical environment. Its optimal growing temperature range is 64°–70°F (18°C–21°C) but it can tolerate up to 73°F (24°C).

Explore these mean annual temperature visualizations for Brazil, Colombia, Honduras, and Ethiopia the largest Arabica coffee producing countries.

Not only are mean temperatures currently past the optimal growing range, these important coffee growing regions are currently experiencing temperatures near or past the maximum growing temperature of 73°F.

Data is from World Bank Group, Climate Change Knowledge Portal

Effects of a warmer climate

Decreasing supply and quality

  • Fruit development and ripening accelerate. Faster ripening degrades coffee bean quality.
  • Consistent exposure to elevated temperatures can damage coffee plants, stunt growth, and bring on disease and exacerbate pests.
  • As temperatures rise, diseases like coffee rust increase. This plant-choking fungus withers trees and slashes production causing exports to plunge their effects rippling through local economies.

Catastrophic effects on farming communities

  • Changing temperature and rainfall can reduce coffee-growing areas and raise the minimum altitude for coffee production.
  • Climate impact is most apparent in Ethiopia where Arabica originated. Projections show that 39–59% of their main growing area could experience climatic changes large enough to make areas unsuitable for coffee. (Moat, 2017)
  • Effects could be catastrophic for coffee growers and their workers. Much of the world’s coffee production depends on farmers living at a minimum level with many only growing coffee. Loss of their coffee-growing income would create significant hardship.

Insight: Adapt and adjust how coffee is grown.

  • Shaded Coffee Plantations

    As suitable farmland for coffee cultivation decreases, some researchers recommend shaded plantations. Shaded coffee plantations are also being proposed as a solution to rising temperatures and increasing pests. Shaded trees protect coffee from extreme temperatures and provide a habitat for animals that could feed on pests like the berry borer.

  • Genetic Development

    Coffee growers are also focusing on genetic improvement selecting coffee strains that show greater tolerance for heat. (“Climate & Coffee | NOAA Climate.gov”)

  • Move to Higher Altitudes

    Coffee growers are also moving coffee fields to higher altitudes and cooler temperatures like in Ethiopia where coffee fields are recommended to move to higher ground because of climate change.

  • Expanding the Coffee Belt

    Climate change is harming crops in numerous locales, but opening up possibilities in other regions like California and Florida, where farmers and researchers are looking at growing coffee. (Person and Teixeira, 2021)
