We would like to extend our sincere gratitude to the research scientists Yanghui Kang and Maoya Bassiouni, of the Keenan Group - Quantitative Ecosystem Dynamics Lab at UC Berkeley. Their invaluable ideas and insights were instrumental in the development of our web application, CarbonTrack - Visualizing Artificial Intelligence Upscaled Ecosystem Dynamics. Their expertise and guidance were critical in shaping the direction of our project and ensuring its success. We are grateful for their generous contribution of time, knowledge, and expertise, which helped us to develop a high-quality tool that will benefit the scientific community and beyond.

We are particularly grateful to Yanghui Kang. CarbonTrack is powered by her work on the CEDAR Project. Her research focuses on the generation of a global GPP dataset that incorporates direct CO2 fertilization to improve the estimation of long-term dynamics. By leveraging machine learning and satellite data to upscale eddy covariance GPP measurements, CEDAR datasets differentiate themselves by incorporating CO2 fertilization effects with a data-driven approach and biophysical theories.
