GPP Data Portal

The GPP Data Portal, a collaborative effort between the UC Berkeley School of Information and the Keenan Group, aims to provide researchers with access to valuable data products related to terrestrial ecosystems and their response to climate variability. Under the guidance of the Keenan Group, Master of Information and Data Science (MIDS) students created a data portal to manage and visualize GPP data generated by machine learning models, with a goal of sharing the data products with researchers globally.

The Google Earth Engine powered web application allows researchers to access the CEDAR datasets to visualize geospatial data to a 0.05-degree spatial resolution. The application includes features such as custom drawing tools to track regions of interest and visualize time-series GPP changes. This web application is a helpful tool for error analysis and allows researchers to compare datasets and models visually.
