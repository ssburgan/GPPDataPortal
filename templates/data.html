CEDAR Data

The CEDAR datasets were generated using novel methods of carbon upscaling using Fluxnet towers and computational upscaling. This work has been created by Yanghui Kang, Phd, a member of the UC Berkeley Keenan Group, and is meant to further the study of GPP modeling. The data models used in this project stem from remote sensing and sensor networks through machine learning and physical modeling.

The input data comes from a number of covariate datasets, including: BESS PAR, MODIS surface reflectance and surface temperature, Continuous SIF, ESA CCI Soil Moisture, ERA5-land, Monthly CO2 concentration, Modis Land Cover, and Koppen climate zones. The data comes in 2 temporal ranges, 3 CFE scenarios, and 2 GPP partitions, all with a spatial resolution of 0.05 degrees.
