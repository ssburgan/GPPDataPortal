What is GPP?

Gross Primary Productivity (GPP) is the rate at which photosynthetic organisms (such as plants, algae, and some bacteria) convert carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) into organic matter through photosynthesis. It represents the total amount of carbon fixed by the plant in a given period of time, typically expressed in units of mass per unit area per unit time (e.g. grams of carbon per square meter per day).

GPP plays a significant role in the global carbon cycle, as photosynthetic organisms remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and store it as organic matter. GPP is a modeled metric which is estimated by applying multiple methodologies - most recently machine learning - to remote sensing data from satellites and eddy covariance data from flux towers.
