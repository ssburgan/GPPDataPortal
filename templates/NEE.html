What is Net Ecosystem Exchange (NEE)?

Net Ecosystem Exchange (NEE) is the balance between the Gross Primary Productivity (GPP) of an ecosystem and its ecosystem respiration (ER). It represents the net amount of carbon dioxide (CO2) that is either absorbed or released by the ecosystem over a given period of time. NEE is expressed in units of mass per unit area per unit time (e.g. grams of carbon per square meter per day).

If NEE is negative, it means that the ecosystem is absorbing more CO2 than it is releasing through respiration, and is therefore a carbon sink. This is the case for many forests and other ecosystems with high levels of photosynthesis. If NEE is positive, it means that the ecosystem is releasing more CO2 than it is absorbing, and is therefore a carbon source. This is the case for many agricultural and urban ecosystems, as well as disturbed or degraded ecosystems with lower levels of photosynthesis.

NEE is an important metric for understanding the carbon balance of ecosystems and their contribution to the global carbon cycle. It is primarily measured using eddy covariance tracked by flux towers.
