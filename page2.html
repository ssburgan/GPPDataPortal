Does processing method affect flavor?

Insight: How processing methods impact coffee.

Although this visual investigates two main forms of processing coffee beans - dry and wet washing, there are a couple more methods including pulped natural processing, and semi-washed processing. Here is a quick guide and explanation of these different processing types:

  • 1. Wet processing: Soaking the coffee cherries right after they are picked and allowing fermentation to take place to separate the bean from the cherry.
  • 2. Dry processing: Drying the coffee cherry in the sun then regulary raking them to remove the dried fruit from the beans.
  • 3. Pulped (semi-dry) processing: Removing various amounts of the fruit parts of the coffee cherry then drying the beans.
  • 4. Semi-washed processing: Using a demucilaging machine to remove the coffee cherry skin and most of its mucilage.

Overall, the washed process of coffee beans correlates to a lower average rating on aroma and aftertaste than that of the dry method.
